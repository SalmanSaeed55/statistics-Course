## Types of Data

### Numerical Data:

| Data Type | Definition                      | Examples                            |
|----------|---------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| **Interval** | Equal intervals, no true zero   | Temperature (°C, °F)                |
| **Ratio**    | Equal intervals, true zero      | Height, weight, time                |
| **Discrete** | Countable, separate values      | Students, cars, population counts   |

### Categorical Data:

| Data Type | Definition                          | Examples                                      |
|----------|-------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| **Ordinal**  | Ordered categories, uneven intervals| Education levels, satisfaction ratings        |
| **Nominal**  | Unordered categories                | Gender, cuisine types, colours                |

### Why this matters
- Some statistical analyses can only be applied to certain data types
- Some visualization techniques are more appropriate for certain data types

---

## Sample vs. Population Data
> **Population Data**: Data collected from every member of a group.
> 
> **Sample Data**: Data collected from a subset of the population.

---

## The Ethics of Making up Data

If you fake Data, you could:
- Lose your job/damage your reputation
- Lose other peoples' job/reputation
- Put people in **_danger_**

### Reasons to Simulate Data:
1. Validate analysis methods
2. Learn advantages/disadvantages of different methods
3. Control signals, noise, and effect sizes
4. Understand your data
5. Think more carefully and critically about your data
6. Improve thinking/programming skills
